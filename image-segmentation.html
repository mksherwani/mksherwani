Image Segmentation and Object Detection

Lesion segmentation in lung CT using unsupervised Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)

Revolutionizing COVID-19 Lesion Detection

This project tackles a critical challenge in modern medical imaging: segmenting infected lung regions in CT scans without relying on annotated data. Traditional supervised learning methods require extensive, expert-labeled datasets — a costly and time-consuming process, especially during global health emergencies.

Our approach leverages CycleGAN, a type of generative adversarial network, to transform infected lung images into their healthy counterparts. The difference between these two representations provides a highly accurate segmentation map — all without a single labeled sample.

We further refined our results using attention-guided masks, allowing the model to focus solely on the infected regions while preserving healthy tissue. This innovation significantly boosts segmentation quality and opens the door for real-world deployment.

Impact & Vision

My contribution to this project reflects deep expertise in Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)-based architectures and unsupervised learning strategies. By circumventing the need for annotated data, this method offers scalable solutions during crisis periods like pandemics, where speed and accuracy are vital.

This research reported competitive metrics across multiple evaluation benchmarks — including Dice Score and Structural Similarity — outperforming several state-of-the-art unsupervised models. Beyond COVID-19, the proposed framework can be generalized to other lesion detection problems across medical imaging modalities.

My long-term vision is to create AI systems that augment radiologists, not replace them — systems that can detect, highlight, and adapt in real-time to clinical feedback. With future iterations, we aim to integrate this model into cloud platforms for real-time diagnosis, supporting global healthcare infrastructures with low-resource access.
